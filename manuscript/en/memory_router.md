# Memory Router

-----

## Ch.1

-----

Today I meant to save.

The router was finished last night. It took three years. Set on the desk it looked the size of an external drive, and what it did was much the same. It saves, and it loads. The one difference: what is saved is memory, and where it is loaded is the past.

The body could not be sent. Only memory could be sent. I built within that limit, and within that limit it was finished.

The lab was small. One desk, one chair, equipment lined along the wall. There was one window, and the blind had been down for three years.

In the morning I sat at the desk and opened the terminal. The router was connected. The status light was on, steady. I looked at it for a moment. The same desk I had seen every day for three years, yet today the desk looked different.

I pressed the save button.

The time stamped on the terminal. Ten twelve a.m. Saved. Now I could come back.

That was all. The body felt nothing. That a copy of my memory had been made somewhere, there was no way to confirm but the time on the terminal. I leaned back in the chair and sat a long while. A morning in which nothing had happened. So quiet that nothing having happened felt strange.

-----

The event happened in the afternoon.

A cup fell. The one set on the edge of the desk, struck by the elbow as I reached for some papers. The cup broke on the floor, and the coffee left in it flowed slowly toward the box of documents. I looked down at it from the chair. On a normal day I would have sighed and gone for a rag.

Not today.

Today's me still had a morning to return to.

I loaded.

To say I opened my eyes is not exact. My eyes had stayed open. I was simply there. In the lab at ten twelve a.m. In the very posture of leaning back in the chair. The terminal's time pointed to morning, and a cup sat on the edge of the desk.

The cup was there, unbroken.

I moved the cup inward. Then I worked until afternoon. Afternoon came. My elbow passed the edge of the desk. There was nothing there.

The cup did not fall.

A laugh came. It came a long while. The sound of laughing alone in an empty lab, and I had no intention of stopping it.

It works.

Three years settled into those two words.

-----

The memory of the broken cup remained.

The sound of it breaking on the floor, the coffee flowing, the impulse to go for a rag. I remembered all of it. It had become a thing that did not happen, yet the memory was as vivid as a thing that had. That was the router's way. Not to erase a day, but to come back carrying the day's memory.

It was not bad. It was better, even. An afternoon lived once. An afternoon where you know where the cup falls. A day whose ending you have seen in advance was safe.

The list of things that could be undone grew in my head. A broken cup. A mis-sent mail. A word I should not have said. A missed timing. They all became things I could go back to.

Saving became a habit. Once in the morning, once before the work began. Only after I confirmed the time stamp on the terminal did the day begin.

That night, before sleep, I wrote tomorrow's tasks. The writing hand had grown faster.

-----

## Ch.2

-----

At first I used it only on big things.

Things broken, things mis-sent, the kind of thing called irreversible. Loading turned such things into things that could be reversed. After about a month the big things grew fewer. Because they could be stopped before they happened.

After that I used it on small things.

When I chose the wrong word. When I took the wrong road. When lunch was no good. Finding a reason not to undo what could be undone was harder than undoing it. When I made a mistake, my hand moved before the thought.

It is not too late yet.

I loaded.

That sentence became a habit of the mouth. A short incantation wedged between mistake and load. It is not too late yet. That phrase was always true. As long as the router existed, "too late" was a concept that did not exist.

-----

Load, and that day is gone. The memory of the gone day remains.

At first those memories were like data. A today lived once. An ending seen in advance. To remember a thing that did not happen was, at first, not strange.

As they piled, it changed.

Load once a day, and two todays form in the head. Twice, three. The today that is lived is one, but the todays remembered were more than that. What happened and what did not happen were remembered at the same brightness. Telling them apart began to take time. At first one second. Then a little more.

In the morning I drank coffee. After drinking it, I learned I had the meeting time wrong. I loaded.

It was morning. I had not drunk coffee. I was not late for the meeting.

The taste of coffee stayed in my mouth.

The taste of coffee I had not drunk. I ran my tongue over the roof of my mouth. The taste was there. The cup was in the cupboard. Dry. I opened the cupboard and checked the cup, and stood a long while between the cup being dry and the coffee taste being in my mouth.

Memory is sent to the past. Sensation comes back stuck to it.

After that such things came. The feel of a handshake not given stayed on my palm. The voice of words not heard stayed in my ear. The height of a chair not sat on stayed in my waist, and the number of stairs not climbed stayed in my legs. Things that had not happened left marks on the body and went. The router was normal. The terminal's record was normal too. The marks remained only where the record had none.

-----

I began to write down what happened today.

On the terminal. Briefly. Time and fact only. What was written was what was real. What was not written but remembered was a thing that had not happened. Deciding it that way made telling apart fast again. When confused, I looked at the terminal. The terminal did not lie. The number of times I checked the terminal grew. At first a few times a day. Later, every time a memory came.

A memory coming at all became a thing to be checked.

It is not too late yet. Not yet.

-----

There was a plant on the windowsill.

I don't know its name. It was a gift. For three years it had grown on its own at the lab window, and I gave it water now and then.

The leaves began to dry.

I gave it water. A few days later it died.

I loaded. I gave it water earlier. It died.

I loaded. I moved it. To where more light fell. It died.

Only the date of death shifted a little.

I did not load again. I looked a long while at the dried leaves. The soil in the pot was wet. On the wet soil the leaves had dried. I knew what I could send. Memory. Memory was not water.

There are things memory, sent, cannot save.

I learned it that day. Not learned with the head, but learned before the pot. There were three memories of the dead plant. All three were today, and all three ended the same.

-----

The number of times I went to the lab dropped.

Going, the things to remember grew. The more things to remember, the more things to check. In the room there was less to do. Less to do, fewer todays.

The room was smaller than the lab. A bed and a terminal and one window. Through the window an alley showed, and seeing the alley made a memory of the alley, so I drew the curtain.

In the room I looked at the terminal. I matched the today written and the today remembered. Matching them took the morning.

I went to the lab. There was a memory of going. There was also a memory of not going. I looked at the terminal. It was not written. Then I had not gone. The feel of the desk was in my hand. The height of the chair I had sat in was in my body. The chair of a lab I had not gone to.

I drank coffee.

I did not drink it.

The taste came.

I went to the meeting. I will go. Did I go.

I looked for the lab key.

It was in my hand.

I looked at the terminal. The memories of looking at the terminal were several. I had to pick which one was just now. I picked. To check the picked one was right, I looked at the terminal.

I thought of what I ate this morning. There were several mornings. All of them were today.

In. The. Morning. Cof. Fee.

I drank it. I did not drink it.

I dr. ank.

Since when I had stopped counting I could not remember.

Not too late. So it seemed.

-----

It was night. That it was night I confirmed through the window.

Whether I had saved I could not remember. There was a memory of saving. Whether that memory was today's I could not tell. I looked at the terminal. A time was stamped. I read the stamped time. Afraid I would forget what I read, I read it again.

The router was on the desk. The light was on. Steady. Just as designed three years ago.

What was broken was not the router.

-----

## Ch.3

-----

It was morning.

That it was the one and only morning took time to confirm. There was a line written last night on the terminal. One line. I read it. Yesterday settled into one. I sat a moment holding the settled yesterday.

Then I went to the desk.

The light was on the router. My hand was over the power button.

I could have saved before turning it off. Then I could come back even after turning it off. Load, and I could think again. I might find a better way. The router kept that path open to the last. That was what the router did.

I did not.

I turned the router off. That was all.

The power took about three seconds to go. Three years to make, and three seconds were enough to turn it off. Once the light died it looked like an external drive again. It became an object on the desk.

There was a plan file on the terminal. Commercialization schedule, projected demand, prototype spec. A file that had grown with the router for three years. I did not open it. I did not delete it either. I just left it.

The one who had used it was me alone. That was enough.

-----

I went outside.

Trying to dig up when I last went out, I stopped. Not needing to dig was what today was.

At the end of the alley was a bakery. The smell of bread. A real smell. A smell I did not need to confirm was not a memory. That I did not need to confirm, I knew after confirming. That too would lessen, little by little.

The signal was red. I waited. While waiting I did nothing. There was nothing to go back and fix, no ending known in advance. It turned green. I crossed.

Someone passed with a dog. The dog looked at me once and passed. That happened once, and ended in one.

Today was one.

That was all I confirmed. In front of the bakery, in front of the signal, in the alley. That today was one was true each time I confirmed it. Tomorrow too would come only once. A tomorrow that came once would go once.

On the way home I bought a cup. A cup that would break if dropped.

It was fine.

I came into the room and set the cup on the inner part of the desk. A spot far from the edge. That much was enough.

It became night. I opened the terminal. I wrote one line of what happened today. After writing, I looked a moment at the screen where the save button had been.

Today I decided not to save.
