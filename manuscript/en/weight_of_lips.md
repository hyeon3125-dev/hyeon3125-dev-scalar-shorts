# The Weight of Lips

-----

When I opened my eyes in the morning it was already heavy.

What was heavy was not clear. My body was fine. I had slept as much as usual. Yet from the moment I opened my eyes there was something I was carrying on my shoulders. It had been there last night, and the night before. During sleep I seemed to set it down for a while, but on waking it was still there. Maybe I had never set it down at all.

I washed and dressed and went out. The same as always. What I carried could not be seen from outside. Not even in the mirror. It had weight but no shape.

-----

The office was a twenty-minute walk.

I passed people on the street. They all looked light. Whether they were actually light I could not know. I too would look light from outside. It was the kind of weight that could not be known by what is seen.

I sat down. I turned on the terminal. Things to handle were on the screen. What I had not handled yesterday was stacked on top, and what came in today stacked on that. I handled them in order. While handling them I forgot the weight. Not forgot — one more layer of work was simply laid over the weight.

-----

The speaker at the next desk crackled.

It was old equipment. It made that sound sometimes. It came briefly and stopped. The person beside me did not look up. The one in front stayed the same. The others seemed not to have heard.

I heard it.

I did not say anything. To say it, someone would have to come and look, and looking takes time, and that time was no one else's. The sound soon stopped. Having stopped, it became a thing that had not happened.

-----

In the afternoon there was a meeting.

A long meeting. There was something to be decided and it was not decided. The meeting drifted toward postponing the decision. As it drifted, one person's face fell for a moment. Only a moment. The look of words pressed down just before leaving the mouth. That person in the end said nothing, and the meeting ended on the side of postponing.

I saw that look.

I did not say anything. What that person had swallowed, I could guess. I could not say I was guessing. To say it would be to pull out what that person had swallowed. That was theirs.

After the meeting, I knew whom the postponed decision would later fall to. Among those present, no one else seemed to have seen even that.

I knew.

I did not say anything.

-----

In the evening he sat beside me.

Someone from the same company. Not someone I often spoke with. He sat, and I was sitting. A drop of water on the window slid slowly down. When it had started falling I did not know.

We were there about ten minutes.

It was ten minutes in which I could have spoken. He was a stranger, and to a stranger one could set anything down. Set it down once and he would have held it. Even briefly. Even for ten minutes' worth.

I said nothing. He said nothing either. He rose first. As he left he gave a slight nod, and I nodded too. He went.

There was nothing I set down. He would not even have guessed. The person who sat beside me for ten minutes did not know what I had nearly set down in those ten minutes.

The meeting time did not change. The decision left unreceived stayed the same.

-----

I returned to my desk.

A mail window was open on the terminal. Something I had begun and left in the daytime. The recipient was written. The content was written. All that was left was to send.

I could have sent it.

I did not send it.

I did not close the window either. I left it open. Leaving it in a state where it could be sent and sending it were different. I left it in a state where it could be sent.

-----

At night I walked home.

The same road as on the way out. At the crosswalk the signal blinked. A person crossing ran. One foot slipped. They nearly fell, and did not fall. That person kept going.

I saw that one foot.

I did not say anything. Because nothing had happened. Nothing having happened was nothing having happened with no one knowing.

The rain had stopped but the wet smell remained. What I carried was the same. Over the day it did not lessen. It did not grow either. Just as new work came in equal to the work handled, so it was with the weight.

The load was familiar.

The weight of lips was not.

I had grown used to what I carried, but the weight of unsaid things piling on the lips I had not grown used to. Each time it felt like new.

The speaker's sound. The fallen face. The slipped foot. The unsent mail.

One is soon forgotten. Four were not.

I came home and turned on the light. I drank water. I sat down.

-----

No one knew.

So no one's permission was needed.

The morning would come again.

-----

*The Weight of Lips — end*
